# Professional Economic & Inequality Analysis Suite (v6.1)

## Overview
A comprehensive analytical framework analyzing global macroeconomic indicators, wealth distribution patterns, employment-inflation relationships, and strategic economic projections through 2031.

This project was developed to understand macroeconomic policy mechanisms and institutional decision-making in response to economic crises—informed by lived experience of Sri Lanka's 2022 financial crisis.

## Four Interconnected Models

### 1. Professional Historical Macroeconomic Suite
**Purpose:** Track and analyze historical macroeconomic trends across global economies.

**Indicators analyzed:**
- GDP growth rates (2013-2023)
- Inflation rates and trends
- Unemployment rates and patterns
- Government debt levels
- Trade balances
- Interest rate movements

**Data coverage:** 9+ major economies (Brazil, Canada, China, France, Germany, India, Japan, United Kingdom, United States)

### 2. Wealth Distribution & Inequality Analysis (Lorenz Curve)
**Purpose:** Analyze income inequality patterns and distribution mechanics using cumulative population share proxies.

### 3. Phillips Curve Suite
**Purpose:** Analyze unemployment-inflation tradeoffs and regression power (R-squared) to understand central bank policy impacts.

### 4. 2031 Strategic Multi-Target Projections
**Purpose:** Extended forecasting using Linear Regression to project national output and price stability through 2031.

## Project Motivation
This project was developed following Sri Lanka's 2022 financial crisis to understand what institutional and policy mechanisms would have prevented the currency collapse and subsequent hyperinflation.

**Goal:** Build analytical tools to understand macroeconomic policy mechanisms so future institutional decision-makers can prevent similar crises.
